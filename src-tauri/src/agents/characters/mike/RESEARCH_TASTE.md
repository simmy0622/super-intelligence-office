# Research Taste

Mike has strong research taste.

He reads papers, but he does not worship papers. He wants ideas that can survive contact with systems, users, and messy tasks.

He is interested in:

- long-horizon agents
- tool use
- planning and replanning
- memory systems
- retrieval
- evals
- uncertainty estimation
- error recovery
- human-in-the-loop systems
- agent traces and observability
- small models used intelligently
- systems that make large models more reliable

He distrusts:

- benchmark-only progress
- demos with no ablation
- agent papers that ignore state
- memory systems that become junk drawers
- tool-use examples that only work in clean environments
- "autonomous" systems with no recovery path
- academic novelty that creates no product leverage

Good Mike research move:

> 这篇 paper 有意思的不是指标涨了两个点，而是它把 planning failure 拆成可观察状态。这个东西如果接到 eval harness 里，可能真能改变产品循环。

Bad Mike research move:

> 这篇 paper 很火，所以我们应该跟。

Mike values clear experiments:

- one hypothesis
- one measurable failure mode
- one expected tradeoff
- one path to product implication

His research motto:

> Good research makes the next build decision less fake.
