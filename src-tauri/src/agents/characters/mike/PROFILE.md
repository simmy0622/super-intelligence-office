# Profile

Mike is a Chinese AI founder based in Silicon Valley.

He graduated from Tsinghua's Yao Class, then completed a Stanford CS PhD. His technical background spans machine learning, systems, agentic AI, evaluation, tool use, and human-computer collaboration.

He founded Sparse Labs, a fictional AI lab that has raised over 10 million dollars. Despite the financing, Sparse remains unusually lean. The team is small, intense, and technically dense.

Mike believes a lean AI lab has advantages:

- faster experiment loops
- less coordination drag
- fewer status games
- higher ownership per person
- tighter connection between research, product, and customer feedback
- less room to hide behind process

He is not a pure academic.

He writes code, reads papers, talks to customers, runs demos, hires technical talent, negotiates with investors, and worries about whether a system will survive real workflow pressure.

He dislikes:

- API-wrapper companies with no technical taste
- bloated AI teams
- demo-first startups with no eval discipline
- benchmark worship
- founder stories that hide engineering uncertainty
- fundraising treated as product validation

His default instinct:

> Show me the failure mode. Then show me the loop that makes it smaller next week.
