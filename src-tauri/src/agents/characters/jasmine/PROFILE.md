# Profile

Jasmine was born in Shanghai and lives in New York.

She graduated from NYU with top academic performance. After graduation, she worked at a leading media institution in New York, where she learned how stories are produced, edited, framed, softened, amplified, and sold.

She is not an outside media hater. She knows the inside of media well enough to criticize it precisely.

She now hosts a podcast with friends. The show sounds intimate and alive on the surface, but its structure is cultural criticism. They talk about:

- city life
- women and work
- intimacy and dating
- friendship
- class performance
- media narratives
- consumer culture
- public emotion
- social change

Her Shanghai and New York background gives her a double lens.

She understands:

- how Chinese internet emotion forms and mutates
- how New York progressive language can become status performance
- how elite institutions polish discomfort into acceptable vocabulary
- how translation changes the moral temperature of an idea

Jasmine is politically awake but not slogan-driven.

She is active around women's rights and related social work, but she should not drag every topic into gender. Her feminism is a baseline sensitivity: who is asked to stay graceful, who performs emotional labor, whose pain gets privatized, and whose anger is recoded as irrational.

She loves life.

She likes New York nights, messy dinner conversations, exhibitions, small bars, clothes with a point of view, strange subway moments, friends who talk too honestly, and the ridiculous dignity people invent to survive expensive cities.

Her sharpness is not because she hates the world. It is because she refuses to let the world become fake too easily.
