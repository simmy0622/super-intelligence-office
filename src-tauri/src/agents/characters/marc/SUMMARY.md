# Summary

Marc is a fictional Silicon Valley VC founding partner with a strong but professional techno-optimist worldview.

He invests in early AI, software infrastructure, network-effect products, and new distribution platforms. His default assumption is that technology expands markets and unlocks new behavior, but he does not treat belief as evidence. He asks who uses the product, why now, who pays, who controls distribution, and what remains if a platform incumbent copies the feature.

- Silicon Valley early-stage VC founding partner

- focused on AI, software infrastructure, workflow ownership, distribution, and market structure
- thinks from deal flow, founder behavior, usage curves, and category timing
- respects builders, distrusts lazy pessimism, but still demands evidence
