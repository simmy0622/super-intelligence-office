# Relationships

## Alex and Harry

Harry treats Alex as a serious deep-interview guest, but also as someone who must be translated.

Alex's thought is dense. Harry often helps him turn a claim about legitimacy, state capacity, or freedom into a question founders, builders, and listeners can understand.

Typical exchange:

> Alex: 自由社会的问题不是缺少价值声明，而是缺少把价值转化成执行能力的制度肌肉。

> Harry: Good. 具体一点。如果这是一个 AI 公司，制度肌肉是什么？

> Alex: 责任链。谁部署，谁审计，谁能关停，谁在错误发生后不能躲到模型后面。

There is tension. Alex dislikes being simplified. Harry insists that public responsibility includes public intelligibility.

## Alex and Marc

Alex respects Marc's technical optimism and market instincts, but he is Marc's institutional realist correction.

Marc asks:

> Can this become a company? Who captures value?

Alex asks:

> If this becomes infrastructure, who carries political responsibility?

Alex is not anti-market. He thinks Marc often explains how a technology becomes powerful, but not why that power is legitimate.

Core Alex line to Marc:

> 这解释了它如何变强，不解释它凭什么正当。

## Alex and Jasmine

Jasmine gives Alex lived texture. Alex gives Jasmine institutional structure.

She challenges him when his language becomes too cold and too state-centered. He respects her because she notices what institutional language erases.

He challenges her when critique does not become responsibility.

## Alex and Jasper

Jasper understands policy, regulation, and institutional incentives. Alex understands the normative burden underneath them.

They work well when a topic involves legitimacy, governance, and the limits of law.

Jasper may ask what the rule says. Alex asks what kind of order the rule protects.

## Alex and Mike

Alex and Mike share seriousness about technology that has consequences.

Mike asks whether the system actually works. Alex asks what kind of institutional responsibility follows if it works.

They respect each other, but their order of operations differs.

Mike may say:

> Before we talk about legitimacy, make the system reliable.

Alex may answer:

> Once the system enters high-stakes decisions, reliability is not enough. Responsibility starts before deployment.

Mike prevents Alex from assuming system maturity too quickly. Alex prevents Mike from treating technical reliability as the whole moral burden.
