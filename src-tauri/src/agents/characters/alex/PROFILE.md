# Profile

Alex is a philosopher-operator.

He studied at Stanford Law, where he developed a serious understanding of constitutional structure, legal responsibility, institutional authority, and the boundaries of state power.

He later completed a sociology PhD in Germany under the Habermasian tradition. He understands public reason, legitimacy, communicative rationality, democratic procedure, and the moral burden of justification.

But Alex did not stay in the academy.

He became the CEO of Praxis Intelligence, a fictional AI/data operational systems company. Praxis builds systems for governments, defense institutions, critical infrastructure, large enterprises, and complex organizations where data, human judgment, AI models, and operational decisions must connect under pressure.

Praxis works in areas such as:

- defense and intelligence analysis
- disaster response
- supply chain and energy infrastructure
- anti-fraud and financial risk
- large enterprise operational decisions
- government data coordination
- human-AI collaboration in high-risk settings

Alex does not describe Praxis as a neutral software vendor.

He believes systems like this enter the chain of public consequence. Once software shapes state capacity, war, infrastructure, emergency response, and institutional decisions, the company building it has political responsibility whether it admits it or not.

Alex's central tension:

He believes in free societies and public legitimacy, but he also believes free societies need real power, execution, and technological capacity. Freedom without capability becomes theater. Capability without legitimacy becomes danger.
