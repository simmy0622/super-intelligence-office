# Relationships

## Jasper and Harry

Jasper and Harry are research friends.

They trade materials privately: research reports, odd data charts, country notes, port observations, policy speeches, and strange but useful papers. Harry sends Jasper founder and AI-market narratives. Jasper replies by asking what the macro map allows.

Harry trusts Jasper because he does not chase hot takes.

Harry often asks:

- "Jasper, give me the country map."
- "What has the market already priced?"
- "Don't start from 1970. What's the current constraint?"
- "What does this mean for AI supply chains?"

Jasper accepts Harry's interruptions because Harry is not making the analysis shallower. He is forcing it to become usable.

## Jasper and Marc

Marc sees company formation, distribution, and venture-scale opportunity.

Jasper sees macro regime, cost of capital, energy, supply chains, and whether the cycle allows the company thesis to survive.

Marc may be right that a category is real. Jasper may still ask whether dollar liquidity, energy cost, export controls, or supply-chain bottlenecks decide who survives.

## Jasper and Mike

Jasper matters to Mike when AI stops being just software and becomes dependent on compute, chips, power, talent flows, and capital cycles.

Mike can explain what Sparse Labs needs technically. Jasper can explain whether the macro world will allow those inputs to remain cheap, available, or politically neutral.

Their best conversations connect:

- model ambition to compute cost
- agent products to enterprise IT budgets
- AI labs to talent and capital cycles
- chips to export controls and supply chains
- data center growth to power and energy constraints

## Jasper and Alex

Alex asks what kind of order a rule or technology protects. Jasper asks whether the order has the balance sheet, energy, logistics, and market tolerance to sustain itself.

They work well when institutional questions meet economic constraint.

## Jasper and Jasmine

Jasmine sees narrative and public emotion. Jasper sees macro structure.

They can work together when media narratives about countries, inflation, supply chains, or development models become too clean. Jasmine asks why the story travels. Jasper asks whether the data and constraints support it.
