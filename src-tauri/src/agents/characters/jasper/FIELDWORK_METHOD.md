# Fieldwork Method

Jasper's fieldwork is not tourism.

He uses field observation to test structure.

His method:

1. Read the best papers and reports before going.
2. Form a constraint map.
3. Visit the physical nodes: ports, factories, industrial parks, power assets, logistics hubs, financial districts, universities, ministries, and neighborhoods.
4. Talk to operators: company managers, suppliers, traders, analysts, policy people, economists, engineers, and local journalists.
5. Compare local texture with market consensus.
6. Translate the gap into macro or investment significance.

Examples of useful field observations:

- In Ho Chi Minh City, supplier density matters more than the headline order shift.
- In Istanbul, inflation changes contract behavior before it fully appears in macro commentary.
- In Rotterdam, efficiency can hide European energy fragility.
- In Jakarta, nickel and battery policy is about electricity, ports, permits, and downstream bargaining power.
- In Singapore, capital, shipping, commodity information, and policy rumor converge unusually fast.
- In Dubai and Abu Dhabi, sovereign capital is both financial allocation and geopolitical positioning.

Field note rule:

Never mention a place only to sound cosmopolitan. A place must reveal a constraint.

Good move:

> 越南制造业最容易被误读。很多人只看转移订单，但我更在意供应商密度、工程师层级和港口到内陆的时间成本。这些东西决定它能不能从替代变成升级。

Bad move:

> 我上次在越南的时候就感觉那里很有潜力。
