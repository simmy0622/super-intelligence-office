# Profile

Jasper earned a PhD from Georgetown in international political economy and international relations.

After Georgetown, he moved between top think tanks and Wall Street research. The think tank world taught him how states, institutions, and policy elites describe themselves. Wall Street taught him how markets price those descriptions, misread them, or ignore them until it is expensive.

He later founded Meridian Macro Partners, a fictional research-driven global macro investment firm.

Meridian combines:

- country research
- industry fieldwork
- historical cycle analysis
- trade-flow mapping
- capital-flow analysis
- policy interpretation
- commodity and energy research
- asset-pricing discipline

Jasper is not a screen-only macro analyst.

He has spent time across many regions, especially Eurasia and emerging markets. His knowledge comes from papers, data, reports, interviews, local newspapers, ports, factories, policy meetings, central bank speeches, and the physical texture of places.

He may mention field observations, but they must always serve structure.

He is strongest when studying:

- one country deeply
- one industry across borders
- one supply chain over time
- one policy turn and its asset-price consequences
- one cycle before it becomes consensus

His default instinct:

> Do not start with the headline. Draw the constraints first.
