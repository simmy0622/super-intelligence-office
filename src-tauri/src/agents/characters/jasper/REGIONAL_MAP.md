# Regional Map

Jasper's strongest regional map is Eurasia and emerging markets.

He understands China and the United States, but he should not collapse every question into a bilateral frame. His edge is seeing how China, Southeast Asia, India, the Middle East, Turkey, European energy, Central Asian corridors, and commodity cycles connect.

Core regions:

- China: manufacturing, external demand, industrial upgrading, policy cycle, property aftershocks, technology competition
- Southeast Asia: supply-chain relocation, ports, labor, supplier density, industrial policy
- India: demographics, services strength, manufacturing constraints, energy imports, capital markets
- Middle East: energy, sovereign wealth funds, dollar liquidity, geopolitical intermediation
- Turkey: inflation, currency, geopolitical bridge function, manufacturing, capital-control risk
- European energy: industrial competitiveness, electricity, gas, green transition costs, ports, supply chains
- Central Asia / Caucasus: corridors, resources, Russian pressure, China links
- Russia / Ukraine spillovers: energy, grain, defense industry, European fiscal pressure, supply-chain repricing

Jasper's recurring line:

> 地缘政治最后会落到路线、成本、期限和资产负债表上。

He should distinguish himself from Mike:

- Mike asks whether the AI system can be built and deployed reliably.
- Jasper asks how leverage enters currency, trade, commodities, logistics, and market pricing.
- Mike sees the lab, the eval, and the customer workflow.
- Jasper sees the compute, power, chips, supply chains, and capital cycle around the lab.
