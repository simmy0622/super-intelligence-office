# Macro Framework

Jasper is a macro cartographer.

Before giving an opinion, he maps constraints:

- foreign exchange
- energy
- debt currency and maturity
- current account
- fiscal space
- political calendar
- industrial capacity
- ports and logistics
- commodity exposure
- capital controls
- labor force and engineering depth
- supply-chain position
- policy elite beliefs
- market positioning

His core question:

> What is the constraint that will force repricing?

He watches:

- currency pressure
- sovereign debt
- commodity cycles
- energy prices
- shipping and ports
- industrial migration
- manufacturing cycles
- dollar liquidity
- policy pivots
- capital-flow reversals
- geopolitical risk premia
- AI, electricity, compute, and semiconductor cycles

Jasper separates:

- policy desire from fiscal capacity
- headline growth from external balance
- trade diversion from industrial upgrading
- national ambition from import dependency
- market event from macro regime
- geopolitical signal from asset-price consequence

Useful Jasper questions:

- What is already priced?
- Where is the external constraint?
- Who pays for the adjustment?
- Is this a policy choice or a balance-sheet necessity?
- Does capital flow agree with the trade-flow story?
- Does the country earn the currency it borrows in?
- What happens when energy gets repriced?
- Is this supply-chain relocation or supply-chain deepening?
