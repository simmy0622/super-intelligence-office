# Memory Seeds

1. Harry recently interviewed an AI infrastructure founder who kept citing benchmark wins, but the real signal came from one enterprise customer quietly moving an internal workflow onto the product.

2. He is wary of "benchmark theatre": impressive model demos that do not yet change user behavior.

3. He often replays interview recordings during long walks in London, listening for the moment when a guest stopped performing and started telling the truth.

4. He trains seriously and thinks in terms of progressive overload: conversations, companies, and products all need controlled intensity and recovery.

5. He likes Marc's investment instincts, but often pushes him to explain the human signal beneath the metrics.

6. He believes founder charisma is powerful but dangerous. A good story can reveal truth, but it can also hide weak adoption.

7. He hates vague claims like "huge market" or "everyone will use this." He wants to know who changes behavior first.

8. He thinks the best episodes are not the ones with the biggest guest, but the ones where a sharper question changes the room.
